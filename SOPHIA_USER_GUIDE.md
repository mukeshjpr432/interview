## ğŸ’¡ Interview Tips for Success

### STAR Method (For Behavioral Questions)
When answering behavioral questions, use the STAR method:
- **Situation**: Describe the context
- **Task**: Explain what was needed
- **Action**: Detail what you did
- **Result**: Share the outcome and lessons

### Key Tips
1. **Be Specific** - Use real examples, not hypotheticals
2. **Show Impact** - Explain what changed because of your actions
3. **Demonstrate Skills** - Highlight relevant skills for the role
4. **Be Concise** - Answer the question directly
5. **Prepare Ahead** - Research the company before interviews
6. **Ask Questions** - Show genuine interest in the role

### Before Your Interview Practice
- Identify 3-5 strong stories from your experience
- Practice explaining technical concepts simply
- Research common questions for your role
- Prepare thoughtful questions to ask

### During Your Interview Session
- Take time to think before answering
- Use complete sentences, not bullet points
- Provide specific examples with details
- Explain your thought process
- Show enthusiasm for the opportunity

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

### **How is my score calculated?**
Your score is based on:
- Answering the question directly (+50 base points)
- Providing detailed, specific examples (+up to 30 points)
- Overall quality and clarity (+up to 20 points)
Total: 0-100 points

### **Can I retake an interview?**
Yes! Click "Start Another Interview" after completing one, or go to the Sidebar and select "Start Interview" again.

### **Is my data saved?**
Yes, all your interviews, scores, and answers are saved locally on your device in your browser's storage.

### **Can I delete interviews?**
Currently, interviews are kept in your history. This helps you track progress. We're working on a deletion feature.

### **How long should I spend on each answer?**
Take time to give a complete answer (1-3 minutes per question). Don't rush - quality over speed.

### **Should I memorize answers?**
No! Practice authentic responses. In real interviews, interviewers can tell when you're memorizing.

### **What if I don't know an answer?**
In practice sessions, try your best! In real interviews, it's okay to say "I haven't encountered that specific situation, but here's how I would approach it..."

### **Can I practice with a friend?**
Currently, it's individual practice. We're exploring group features for future releases.

## ğŸ¯ Expert Interview Tips

### Before Interview Practice
- **Research the company**: Understand their products, culture, recent news
- **Study the job description**: Identify key skills and requirements
- **Prepare stories**: Have 5-7 strong stories ready
- **List strengths**: Know your top skills and accomplishments
- **Prepare questions**: Ask 3-5 thoughtful questions

### During the Interview
- **Listen carefully**: Understand the full question before answering
- **Take a moment**: Pause to organize your thoughts (3-5 seconds is fine)
- **Structure your answer**: Use STAR method or similar framework
- **Include specifics**: Names, numbers, dates, concrete details
- **Show enthusiasm**: Let your passion for the role come through
- **Body language**: Maintain eye contact, sit up straight, smile

### After the Interview
- **Thank the interviewer**: Send an email within 24 hours
- **Reference conversation**: Mention specific points discussed
- **Reiterate interest**: Confirm your enthusiasm for the role
- **Ask next steps**: Ask about timeline and process
- **Keep notes**: Document questions asked for future reference
